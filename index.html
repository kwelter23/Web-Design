Summary: Latitude vs. X

A number of variables contribute to the climate of a city. The latitude of a city is likely to impact the city’s weather. For example, different latitudes receive different amounts of solar radiation. Latitudes closer to the equator receive the highest amounts of solar radiation. It may be obvious that high amounts of solar radiation result in increased temperatures. However, what other aspects of weather are affected by solar radiation or the location of the city’s latitude?

The purpose of this project is to investigate how different aspects of a city’s weather are related to the degree of latitude. This project examines weather data for cities all over the globe of varying degrees of latitude. Weather factors included temperature, humidity, cloudiness and wind speed. On the site you will find multiple scatterplots comparing weather factors to latitude as well as a table of all of the data used.
